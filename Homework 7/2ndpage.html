My favorite story

Kokuhou

"Kokuhou" is a two-part novel by Yūichi Yoshida, based on his three-year undercover stage production research with kabuki actor Umehara Kōzō. It blends the worlds of yakuza and kabuki, following the turbulent life of a man whose fate is shaped by blood ties, rivalry, and the pursuit of artistic excellence.

Overview

The story is divided into "Youth" (Chūsei) and "Path to the Stage" (Hana-michi). It spans the protagonist’s journey from a yakuza family in Nagasaki to becoming a kabuki actress, navigating rivalries, scandals, and the pressures of fame.

Youth (Chūsei)

Set in Nagasaki in 1964, the protagonist Tachibana Kōki is the son of a yakuza family. His striking beauty draws attention, but a violent incident leads him to enter the kabuki world. He is taken in by Kanami Hanaichirō, a leading kabuki actor from the Danboya family, and raised alongside his son Takumi. Initially rivals, they grow close, each pushing the other to excel in kabuki.

Path to the Stage (Hana-michi)

Kōki’s life changes when Hanaichirō, injured in a car accident, appoints Kōki as his understudy for the lead role in Sōgenzakushi. This decision fractures the relationship with Takumi, who leaves Nagasaki with his lover, leaving Kōki to continue his training PIKARINE. The narrative then follows Kōki’s rise through kabuki, film, and television, facing deep loneliness, betrayals, and the moral complexities of the entertainment.
